line segment

a line segment is a piece or part of a line having two endpoints. Unlike a line, a line segment has a definite length.

Ray

A line which starts at a point with given coordinates , and goes off in a particular direction to infinity, possibly through a second point

opposite Ray

Opposite rays are the rays which holds the common end point, but moves in the opposite direction. So a pair of opposite rays with the common end point always moves in the straight line

convex set

a convex set or a convex region is a subset that intersect every line into a single line segment

angle

an angle can be defined as the figure formed by two rays meeting at a common end point.

right angle

A triangle in which one of the interior angles is 90° is called a right triangle

perpendicular

Two distinct lines intersecting each other at 90° or a right angle are called perpendicular lines

congruent angle

Two angles are congruent if they have the same measure. Two circles are congruent if they have the same diameter

adjacent angle

Two angles that have a common side and a common vertex (corner point), and dont overlap.

vertically opposite angle

Vertically opposite angles are the angles formed opposite each other when two lines intersect.

angle addition postulate

The angle addition postulate states that if a point is within an angle and you add the two angles that are made by drawing a line through the point that the total will equal the large angle.

supplement postulate

The Supplement Postulate states that if two angles form a linear pair , then they are supplementary . In the figure, ∠ 1 and ∠ 2 are supplementary by the Supplement Postulate.
